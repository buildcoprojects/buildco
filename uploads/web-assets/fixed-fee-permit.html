Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the permit process typically take?

Timelines vary based on project complexity and council workload, but our packages typically result in approvals 40% faster than industry standards. Residential extensions usually take 6-8 weeks, new dwellings 10-12 weeks, and complex projects 16-20 weeks. Our established relationships with local authorities and comprehensive preparation help minimize delays.

What's included in the fixed fee?

Our fixed fee covers all aspects of the permit application process, including documentation preparation, council submissions, stakeholder coordination, RFI responses, and ongoing management until final approval. The only additional costs would be for statutory fees paid directly to councils or authorities, which we'll clearly identify upfront. There are no hidden charges or surprise fees.

What happens if my permit application is rejected?

While our thorough preparation process minimizes rejection risks, if your application is rejected, we'll manage the resubmission process at no additional cost. Our fixed fee covers the entire journey until approval, including any necessary revisions, appeals, or resubmissions. We're committed to achieving successful outcomes for your project.

Do I need to provide anything for the permit application?

You'll need to provide basic project information, property details, and any existing architectural drawings or plans. If you're working with an architect, we'll coordinate directly with them to obtain necessary documentation. For projects without existing plans, we can recommend trusted architectural partners or work with your preferred professionals. We handle all technical documentation, council interactions, and specialized reports.
