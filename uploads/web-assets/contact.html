Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services and processes.

What types of projects do you manage?

We manage a diverse range of projects including high-end residential renovations, commercial office fit-outs, retail spaces, new home constructions, and public infrastructure projects. Our expertise spans across residential, commercial, and civil construction sectors, allowing us to handle projects of various sizes and complexities.

How do your fixed fee permit packages work?

Our fixed fee permit packages provide a transparent, all-inclusive price for handling your permit approval process. We take care of all documentation, council liaisons, and technical requirements to secure your permits. The fee is established upfront based on your project type and complexity, giving you cost certainty and eliminating unexpected expenses. Visit our Fixed-Fee Permit Approval page for specific package details and pricing.

What areas do you service?

We primarily serve clients in Melbourne and the surrounding areas in Victoria. Our team has extensive experience with local councils and regulations in these regions. For projects outside these areas, please contact us to discuss how we can best support your needs.

What is the typical timeline for a project?

Project timelines vary significantly based on scope, complexity, and permit requirements. Residential renovations might take 3-6 months, while larger commercial projects can extend to 12+ months. During our initial consultation, we'll provide a detailed timeline specific to your project. Our team works diligently to maintain schedules and communicate any adjustments promptly.

How do you manage project budgets?

Budget management is a core part of our project approach. We establish comprehensive budgets at the outset, incorporating all known costs and contingencies. Throughout the project, we use robust monitoring systems to track expenses, providing regular updates and alerts if adjustments are needed. Our transparent approach ensures you're always informed about your project's financial status.
